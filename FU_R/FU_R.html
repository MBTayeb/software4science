R is a programming language very commonly used for data analysis and rather established in some fields like statistics, psychology, finance, linguistics, biology, geo informatics, etc. R is free and openly available, and can be used either on the command line or via integrated development environments such as RStudio or via Jupyter Notebooks.

Online resources:

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yZ0bV2Afkjc
-- "Learn R in 39 minutes" by Prof. Andrew Gard, Lake Forest College, IL, USA.
This tutorial introduces Rstudio and shows the most important commands in R.
http://swcarpentry.github.io/r-novice-gapminder/
-- "R for Reproducible Scientific Analysis" course by the Software Carpentry
This self-guided course introduces the basic concepts of programming needed for data analysis with R. It offers challenges that lets you practice what you have learned.
https://swirlstats.com/students.html
-- "Swirl: Learn R, in R"
This R package lets you learn R in the R console. It offers interactive lessons and quizzes.
https://cs50.harvard.edu/r/
-- "CS50’s Introduction to Programming with R" course in the Harvard OpenCourseWare.
This complete university course is for those who prefer watching recorded lectures. One can take the course for free or pay to get a certificate of completion .
https://www.uni-muenster.de/IT/en/services/lehre/lehrveranstaltungen/r.php
-- "Introduction to R" course offered by the CIT, the Center for Information Technology of the University of Münster.
This course is taught in person but should be booked in advance. It is free for students and staff of the University of Münster and gives 3 ECTS points.

Tutorials for R in biology:
https://www.rforbiologists.org/
-- R for Biologists by Prof. Hüseyin Koçak in the University of Miami.
"A collection of episodes with videos, codes, and exercises for learning the basics of the R programming language through examples."
It uses the R console (no RStudio)
https://r-bio.github.io/01-intro-R/
-- "R programming for biologists" class by François Michonneau in the University of Florida.
This self-guided course requires the installation of R and RStudio.
https://r-bootcamp.netlify.app/
-- R-Bootcamp by the Oregon Health & Science University.
This self-guided course requires the installation of R. It made for people who already have some experience with R. It focuses on data visualization and data wrangling.

Tutorials for R in geo informatics:

https://live.osgeo.org/it/overview/R_overview.html
-- "R Quickstart" by the OSGeo Live project
The OSGeo Live is an operating system based on Xubuntu that automatically comes with a variety of open-source geospatial software (including R). The tutorial expects that you have the OSGeo Live running. But you can also do the tutorial on a different R installation.
https://rspatial.org/index.html
-- 'Spatial Data Science with R and “terra”' course by Rspatial (the developers of the terra package and many other geospatial packages).
This course introduces R from scratch and teaches how to work with the terra package for spatial data analysis.
https://geocompr.robinlovelace.net/
-- "Geocomputation with R" book by Robin Lovelace, Jakub Nowosad, Jannes Muenchow.
This focuses on "geographic data analysis, visualization and modeling" with R. It expects good knowledge of R.
