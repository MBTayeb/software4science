To access JupyterLab or another web app running within the container, it is necessary to publish ports from the container to the host system. To understand this, some background in networking is needed. An IP adress is the identifier of a computer in a network. But when one wants to share data between two entities within a network, we want it to go exactly where it is needed, that is why we need ports. Ports allow the system to specify the origin and the destination of data. Since the container is isolated from the computer, they can't share information. Publishing a port binds the port in the container to another one and thus allows everybody in the network to access the service, unless a firewall forbids it.
